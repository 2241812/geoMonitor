Protecting Our Lifelines

The Cordillera Administrative Region serves as the watershed cradle of Northern Philippines, supplying vital water resources to the lowlands. The Department of Environment and Natural Resources' mandate is to monitor, protect, and sustain these ecological boundaries that support millions of Filipinos.

A watershed is an area of land where all water drains to a common outlet. Healthy watersheds mean clean water, productive fisheries, and resilient communities. Through systematic monitoring, we track changes in land use, water quality, and biodiversity across CAR's 14 major watersheds.

14 Major Watersheds of CAR

🏔️

Agno River Basin

One of the largest river systems in the Philippines, spanning Benguet, Ifugao, and Pangasinan.

🌊

Chico River Basin

A major tributary of the Cagayan River, flowing through Kalinga and Mountain Province.

⛰️

Abra River Basin

The largest river system in the Abra province, draining the western slopes of the Central Cordillera.

🌿

Amburayan River Basin

Drains the southern slopes of the Cordillera, flowing through Benguet and La Union.
