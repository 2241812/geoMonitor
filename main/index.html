Protecting Our Lifelines

The Cordillera Administrative Region serves as the watershed cradle of Northern Philippines, supplying vital water resources to the lowlands. The Department of Environment and Natural Resources' mandate is to monitor, protect, and sustain these ecological boundaries that support millions of Filipinos across Regions I, II, and III.

A watershed is an area of land where all water drains to a common outlet such as a river, lake, or sea. Healthy watersheds mean clean water, productive fisheries, and resilient communities. Through systematic monitoring, we track changes in land use, water quality, and biodiversity across CAR's major river basins.

Major River Basins of CAR

The Cordillera Administrative Region hosts 13 major river basins, making it the Watershed Cradle of Northern Luzon — supplying water for irrigation, hydropower, and domestic use far beyond its borders.

🏔️

Agno River Basin

Originates in Benguet, supports the Ambuklao, Binga, and San Roque hydroelectric plants, and supplies irrigation for the Province of Pangasinan.

🌊

Chico River Basin

Headwaters in Benguet, Mountain Province, and Kalinga. Supports mini-hydro plants in Mountain Province and irrigates Kalinga, Apayao, Cagayan, and Isabela.

⛰️

Abra River Basin

Originates from Abra, Mountain Province, and Benguet. One of the five major rivers in the Philippines, providing irrigation for Ilocos Sur.

🌿

Abulog River Basin

Headwaters in Apayao, provides irrigation for the Province of Cagayan, and exits via the Cagayan River to the Babuyan Channel.

🌄

Amburayan River Basin

Emanates in Benguet, flows towards the Luzon Sea at La Union, and supplies irrigation water for the Province of La Union.

🏞️

Aringay River Basin

Originates from Benguet and Baguio City. A source of irrigation for La Union Province, exiting at the Luzon Sea.

💧

Naguilian River Basin

Headwaters in Benguet, provides irrigation water for La Union, and exits at the Luzon Sea.

🌲

Magat River Basin

Originates from Ifugao, supports the Magat Dam hydroelectric plant, and irrigates Isabela, Nueva Vizcaya, and Quirino.

🌾

Siffu-Mallig River Basin

Headwaters in Ifugao and Mountain Province, flows towards the Cagayan River, and sustains irrigation for the Province of Isabela.

🪨

Cabicungan River Basin

Situated in Apayao, supports irrigation for farmers in Cagayan Province and exits towards Claveria.

🌊

Zumiqui-Ziwanan River Basin

Emanates in Apayao, supports irrigation for the Province of Cagayan, and exits towards the Pamplona River.

🌳

Silag River Basin

Located in Abra, supports irrigation, fishing, and serves as a source of potable water, exiting at the Luzon Sea in Ilocos Sur.

🏔️

Upper Cagayan River Basin

The headwaters of one of the largest river systems in the country, gathering from the eastern slopes of the Cordillera and flowing through the Cagayan Valley.
